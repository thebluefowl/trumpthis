The missiles flew. The cities burned.

No victor stood. No lesson learned.

 

The generals spoke of acceptable loss,

of strategic gains and lines to cross.

But the ashes don't know who was right.

The wind carries no flag tonight.

 

Seven billion voices, silenced as one.

Not by God. Not by nature. Not by the sun.

By men in rooms with maps on walls,

who pressed the buttons when reason falls.

 

The earth will heal. In a thousand years,

the forests will return, absent our fears.

But no one will remember what we were,

or why we chose to end it here.

"The living will envy the dead."
